Technical Experience

PAIELLA GROUP - (Boston, MA) - Research Assistant (Sept 2013 – PRESENT)
Project: Plasmonic Meta-Surface directional light emitters and sensors.

- I designed and fabricate optical plasmonic meta-surfaces for directional light sensing and directional light beaming.
- With the directional sensors, in collaboration with a computational imaging group I am designing and building a flat lens-less camera, based on the compound eye vision modality. This camera technology has superior capabilities including infinite depth of view, wide viewing angle and ability for polarized vision.
- I also built a custom automated experimental test setup for testing the finished devices.
- In addition to research, I upgraded our research group website completely changing the design to a more open, easily accessible form and currently still maintain the website.

SCHLUMBERGER-DOLL RESEARCH - (Cambridge, MA) - Research Associate (August 2012 – Present)
Project: High-temperature High-pressure Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) flow sensing.
- I built and tested a prototype circuit board including PCB circuit board design for downhole well sensing tools.
- I also designed and built a high temperature, high pressure test setup appropriate to simulate a real oil rig downhole environment for device testing.

LONCAR LAB. FOR NANO-SCALE OPTICS - (Cambridge, MA) - Research Assistant (June 2010 – May 2012)
Project: Silicon Photonic Crystal ultra-high resolution micro-spectrometer.
- Under guidance of Prof. Loncar and Dr. Parag I designed and built a prototype functional micro-spectrometer based on silicon photonic crystals and micro-heaters. This device was able to recover a spectroscopic spectrum of a standard gas cell with comparable spectral resolution to a conventional bench top high resolution spectrometer, despite being more than 1000 times smaller in dimension.
- I did the fabrication, packaging (including wire bonding) and tested the working device.

BOSTON UNIVERSITY - (Boston, MA) – Optical Processing Facility Lab Assistant(August 2017 – Present)
- I trained new users for various pieces of equipment in the clean-room, helped maintain equipment in good running condition and also helped troubleshoot whenever other cleanroom users had problems running their processes.
- I also wrote a python programming language based GUI instrument driver for the Agilent HP4284A LCR meter to ease data collection for cleanroom users.
- In addition I also initiated and spearheaded upgrade of the cleanroom computers system, procuring new computers and also creation of a cleanroom virtual folder for quick access to reference material and equipment documents f all cleanroom users.
