Technical Experience

PAIELLA GROUP - (Boston, MA) - Research Assistant (Sept 2013 – PRESENT)
Project: Plasmonic Meta-Surface directional light emitters and sensors.
As part of my PhD research, I design and fabricate meta-surfaces tailored to enable directional sensing and directional beaming of light. The goal of this research is to combine these directional sensors to build an ultra-thin, flat lens-less cameras, based on the compound eye vision modality. Cameras based on this technology have superior capabilities including infinite depth of view, wide viewing angle and ability for polarized vision. I do all the design and fabrication including Finite Domain Time Difference optical numerical simulations, Nano-lithography and experimental verification of prototypes. To ease my testing, I write custom LABVIEW programs to automate data collection and allow communication between different types of equipment in from different manufacturers. I also write custom scripts in MATLAB and Lumerical software to automate data processing. With this, I have automated almost every step of my data acquisition and processing making it less prone to measurement error and freeing my time to enable parallel running of different tasks I may have. In addition to my research, I upgraded the group website completely changing the design to a more open, easily accessible form by coding in HTML, JavaScript and CSS. I currently still maintain the website.
SCHLUMBERGER-DOLL RESEARCH - (Cambridge, MA) - Research Associate (August 2012 – Present)
Project: High-temperature High-pressure Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) flow sensing.
As part of the Physics group at SDR, I built and tested a prototype circuit board for downhole well sensing tools. With iterative testing and redesign I was able to shortening the settling time and lower circuit noise which is critical for accurate collection of the weak signals associated with the downhole environment. I did PCB circuit board design, assembled a prototype (including soldering, wiring and programming of microcontrollers). As part of this project, I also designed and built a high temperature, high pressure test setup appropriate to simulate real oil rig downhole environment for the devices.
LONCAR LAB. FOR NANO-SCALE OPTICS - (Cambridge, MA) - Research Assistant (June 2010 – May 2012)
Project: Silicon Photonic Crystal ultra-high resolution micro-spectrometer.
As part of my research in reconfiguration of optical Nano-Cavities for applications in on chip spectroscopy, under guidance of Prof. Loncar and Dr. Parag I developed a prototype functional micro-spectrometer. Using silicon photonic crystals and micro-heaters, this spectrometer was able to recover a spectroscopic spectrum of a standard gas cell with comparable spectral resolution to a conventional bench top high resolution spectrometer, despite being more than 1000 times smaller in dimension. I did the fabrication, packaging (including wire bonding) and testing of the working device.
