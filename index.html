About Me

I am a passionate innovator, always looking for ways to do things more efficiently. I enjoy tinkering around with devices and thoroughly enjoy the creative process of problem solving. I find it especially exciting if solutions for problems require concerted multifaceted approaches spanning different fields and do not have one clear way of approaching them.

Born and raised in Kenya, I am currently a Ph.D. Candidate in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Boston University. My research interests are in the development of novel optoelectronic devices based on artificially-structured material systems. By appropriately tailoring material properties materials can be engineered for specific applications in a way not possible in bulk materials. My main work is in designing Plasmonic meta-surfaces for directional light emission or sensing.
